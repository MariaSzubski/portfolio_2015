JCPenney's FindMore™ is a 40-inch in-store touch screen kiosk that helps . This storefront concept acts as a screensaver and encourages customers to interact with the FindMore. It also highlights shopping departments available on the kiosk. The elements are grouped based on functionality with a compoisition that is intuitive and easy to navigate when users are standing in front of the kiosk. A large format touch screen presents a unique channel. The usability and interpretation of the designs is very different on a landscape monitor vs standing in front of the kiosk. I was able to test my designs on the prototype. Along with the other co-ops, our goal was to develop a single look and feel that could scale to many types of content and backgrounds. We met weekyl with the Innovation team to present and evaluate our ideas. We designed concepts for the browsing and product pages, long with the shopping cart and check out screen. This motion test describes the funcitonality of a storefront concept. This is the screen that users would see as the walk past the kiosk in the store. Interative banners scroll past the middle of the screen, while the breadcrumbs and shopping bag help to indicate funcitonality and interactivity and purpose. Design elements to focus on in the final version: sense of depth (with blur effects, dimensional buttons) color palette that can adapt to various backgrounds emphasis on product imagery guided naviation ? and shopping bag that can be accessed from anywhere in the application
