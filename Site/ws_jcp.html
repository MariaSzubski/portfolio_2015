Background & Purpose

JCPenney's FindMore™ is a 40-inch touch screen kiosk that allows in-store shoppers to compare and purchase products that are out of stock or online-only.

I worked with four other co-op students and the JCPenney Innovation Team to design concepts for the Storefront, product browsing and item detail screens. Our goal was to develop a single look and feel that could scale to any type or amount of content.

Design Details

Concept

The Storefront design acts as a screensaver and encourages shoppers to interact with the FindMore™.

The interactive banners at the top highlight the style guide and latest sales. The product navigation and shopping cart remain visible so that users can access them right from the home screen.

Considerations

All FindMore™ layouts have a sense of depth, an adaptable color palette, and an emphasis on imagery. The guided navigation has to be accessible from any screen.

Storefront design elements are grouped based on functionality within a composition that is intuitive as soon as shoppers walk up to the kiosk.

Challenges

The usability and design on a large-format, portrait touch screen is very different when scaled down on a landscape monitor compared with being viewed on kiosk. I had to account for the height of the user and their viewing angle in each design. The primary content had to stay in the middle 60% of the screen to be readable and tappable to most users.

Final Deliverables

The Storefront animated prototype shows the user experience and interactive features of the Storefront concept.
