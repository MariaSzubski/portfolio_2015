BACKGROUND & PURPOSE This project was a collaboration by the Spring 2011 Digital Design Junior class at the University of Cincinnati's College of DAAP. We worked in six teams to create motion narratives to describe, recount, and recreate the experience of the Urban Walls project. This motion piece focuses on the wall designed by Gordon Salchow. CONCEPT In the 1970s Carl Solway and Jack Boulton organized a public art project called Urban Walls: Cincinnati. The project entailed the commissioning, design, and painting of large mural super graphics on the sides of buildings in downtown Cincinnati. Currently only two of the original murals remain. Gordon Salchow's design was initially intended to be painted on a series of three walls around the Skywalk located at 6th and Race Streets. The wall was painted on the back of the Mabley & Carew building, one of many wall surfaces that were exposed along the West 4th Street district during the early 1970's. Focus: how public art can benefit the community. In the video, we compare the Urban Walls project with current art projects in Cincinnati, including Artworks and Keep Cincinnati Beautiful. The style of the transitions is inspired by the microfilm articles we found during our research. This is meant to link the Urban Walls segment with the Future Blooms project that is going on today. CONSIDERATIONS (keep in mind) We wanted to link the original Urban Walls project with the public art projects in Cincinnati today. ///Forty years later, public art projects continue to be a component of urban revitalization in neighborhoods throughout Cincinnati. CHALLENGES We found original prints from the project, along with news paper articles at the local library. During our research we also walked through downtown and took photos of other wall projects.
