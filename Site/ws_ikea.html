IKEA RFID Scanner re-thinks the IKEA shopping experience, using an electronic shopping list and re-designed in-store navigation. The scanner making it easier to navigate the store and find items in the warehouse. This was a team project with Matt Denzer. The video highlights the features of this design concept. The following slides analyze problems within the current IKEA shopping experience. Our process began with an initial visit to IKEA, both to take photos and observe the existing shopping experience.During our visit, we isolated several problem areas, and noted what IKEA seemed to do well. Goals: wayfinding motion grahics ux design --- Our area of improvement incuded the maps, the room design kiosks, the self serve warehouse process, and the price check stations. We combined our observations on the user experience into this flow chart. *****ADD FLOW CHART***** ---- The first round of design focused on spatial analysis. We chose to go with an isometric view of the store, focusing on both the micro and macro levels of the current user experience. ---- Once the initial analysis was complete. We made recommendations for improvement. Our re-imagined experience focuses on RFID devices that users carry through the store to track their shopping lists. The can quickly add and remove items on the device or view more details about the products they've scanned on kiosks located throughout the store. The motion graphic piece details two complete user experiences. The first focuses on the user who wants to browse and compare. The second focuses on a user who has a product in mind and wants to quickly jump to that section and then move on to the checkout. ***which is first?***
